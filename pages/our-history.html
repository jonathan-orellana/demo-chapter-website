Our Roots, Our Journey: The Story of Lambda Sigma Upsilon

Lambda Sigma Upsilon’s story reflects resilience, unity, and cultural pride—growing from humble beginnings into a strong force for change. Discover our roots, key milestones, and the legacy we continue to build for future generations.

The Struggles of the Late 1970s

Late 1970s: A Time of Struggle and Awakening

Latino students across U.S. universities faced marginalization and began organizing for representation, identity, and equality.

1978: Seeds of Change at Rutgers

Latino students at Rutgers University, especially at Livingston College, protested and united to demand support and recognition.

1979: Building the Brotherhood

1979 (Spring) — Building the Brotherhood Students began meeting regularly to create a space focused on academic success, cultural identity, and community.

April 5, 1979

Students began meeting regularly to create a space focused on academic success, cultural identity, and community.

Lambda Sigma Upsilon Latino Social Fellowship was officially established at Rutgers University, grounded in unity and brotherhood.
